Here we see the top 10 countries (with a population of more than 10 million) that have experienced the largest growth in tertiary enrollment since 1971, all of which are high income countries.

None of these countries exceeded 20% enrollment in 1971, with Australia being the highest at 17% and Saudi Arabia the lowest at 1.6%. While most experienced a linear growth during this period, three countries experience an exponential growth.

Greece, due to persistent high unemployment rates and no tuition at its universities, has become the country with the highest tertiary gross enrollment rates, going from 51% enrollment in 2000 to 137% enrollment in 2017.

Australia, through a combination of university consolidations, fee deferrals and international student recruitment see its enrollment numbers start to exponentially rise in the 1990s.

Finally, Saudi Arabia goes from only 4 tertiary institutes in 1971 to 55 tertiary institutes in 2017, with many opened in the mid-2000s as the result of an oil boom. This expansion results in more available seats meeting the increasing demands of a quickly developing society.
